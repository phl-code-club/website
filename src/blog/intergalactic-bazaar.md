Hey everyone,

If you had asked me a couple years ago while attending Philly Tech Week if I
could imagine the idea of hosting an event for the occasion, I honestly would’ve
laughed, like who? ... yet fast forward to 2026, PHL Code Club hosted our largest
event on May 9th with 50 people in the building (thank you to [IndyHall](https://indyhall.org/) for allowing
four nerds to rent out the space for a whole day).

As [Taj](/organizers/taj) puts it,

> "Our planning usually consist of some pizzas, stickers and the activity itself.
> This event brought on a new meaning to the term: logistics."

We had food, drinks, snacks, prizes, and plenty of swag. From carefully balancing
trays of 60 sandwiches to driving to NJ for discounted Costco goods. We really came
together as a team to pull this off. And it turned out amazing! Our efforts were
evident the day of the CTF, the event ran so smoothly, we were all a bit surprised.
Not even the common HDMI compatibility hiccup during presentation.

And we can all agree it was well worth it! To create a space where people can
relax, learn, make new friends, and be rewarded for their efforts - it’s hard
to put into words the high you get seeing it all unfold. It’s magical. And to
hear from the community, they enjoyed attending just as much as we enjoyed putting
it together, means the world to us.

In [Graham’s](/organizers/graham) words,

> "We had such a great mix of familiar and new faces. I’ve also heard so much
> super positive feedback. It seems like people are hungry for these interactive
> non-standard events, and we plan to continue cooking for them!"

We’re seriously thankful for everyone who came together to make this happen.
